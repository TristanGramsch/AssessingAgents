Tralaland — Assessment, Appraisal & Taxation
=============================================

All monetary figures are in TRL (Tralaland Credits).
All parcel references join on parcel_id from the three source files.


PART I — LEGAL LANDSCAPE
-------------------------

1. Governing Statutes

   Tralaland Property Assessment Act (TPAA)
     - Requires every parcel to carry an assessed value (AV) refreshed on
       a fixed cycle (see Part II).
     - AV is the single legal basis for all ad valorem property taxes.

   Tralaland Revenue Code §§ 400-480 (TRC)
     - Sets the annual mill rate (tax per 1,000 TRL of AV).
     - Current base mill rate: 12.5 mills (= 1.25 % of AV per year).
     - Authorises surcharges: Flood Risk Surcharge (parcels flagged
       flood_risk: true in buildings.cityjson) adds 1.5 mills.

   Land Registration Ordinance (LRO)
     - Governs the recording of deeds (the "instrument" field in
       social.yaml).
     - Every ownership transfer must be registered before the Assessor
       may update the owner of record.
     - Requires disclosure of sale price; that price becomes a key input
       to the next assessment cycle.

   Zoning & Land Use Code (ZLUC)
     - Ties permitted use to zone (residential, commercial, mixed,
       industrial, park/civic) as mapped in geography.geojson.
     - Determines which exemption classes are available (§ 3 below).

2. Assessment Basis

   Tralaland uses a market-value standard: AV shall not exceed the
   price a willing buyer and willing seller would agree upon in an
   arm's-length transaction.  The sales_history in social.yaml is the
   primary evidence base.  Where sales are sparse (e.g. P3, P5),
   the Assessor uses income capitalisation (commercial) or the cost
   approach (industrial/civic).

3. Exemptions (TPAA §§ 70-95)

   Homestead Exemption
     - Owner-occupied residential parcels where owner_type == "person"
       and the owner resides at the address.
     - Reduces AV by 15 % before tax calculation.
     - Current eligible parcels (person owners): P1, P2, P4, P9, P10,
       P11, P12, P16, P17, P18, P20 (subject to residency verification).

   Non-Profit / Civic Exemption
     - Parks and school parcels in geography.geojson are fully exempt.

   Developer Deferral
     - Parcels whose current_owner carries role: "developer" (O5, O7,
       O29) are assessed at land value only until a certificate of
       occupancy is issued.  O7 currently holds P5.

4. Appeal Rights

   Any owner of record may contest an assessment within 60 days of the
   Notice of Assessment.  The three-tier process is detailed in Part II.


PART II — THE ASSESSOR'S OFFICE
--------------------------------

1. Structure

   Chief Assessor (appointed by the Tralaland Municipal Council, 4-year term)
     |
     +-- Deputy Assessor — Residential Division
     |       Handles zones: residential, mixed-use
     |       Primary parcels: P1, P2, P4, P7, P9, P10, P11, P12,
     |                        P13, P15, P16, P17, P18, P20
     |
     +-- Deputy Assessor — Commercial / Industrial Division
     |       Handles zones: commercial, industrial
     |       Primary parcels: P3, P5, P6, P8, P14, P19
     |
     +-- GIS & Data Unit
     |       Maintains geography.geojson and buildings.cityjson.
     |       Flags flood_risk, building condition, and square footage
     |       used as AV adjustment factors.
     |
     +-- Registry & Records Unit
             Syncs with the Land Registration Ordinance.
             Updates current_owner and sales_history in social.yaml upon
             deed recording.
             Issues parcel_id for new lots.

2. Assessment Cycle

   Tralaland operates a triennial (3-year) mass appraisal cycle.

   Year 1 — Data Collection
     - Field inspectors verify building condition (buildings.cityjson
       "condition" field) and flag material changes (additions,
       demolitions, flood damage).
     - Registry & Records Unit reconciles all deeds recorded since the
       last cycle and updates social.yaml.

   Year 2 — Valuation Modelling
     - Statistical mass appraisal model is run using:
         a) Arm's-length sales from sales_history (weight: 60 %)
         b) Building attributes: use, area_sqm, stories, condition
            (weight: 25 %)
         c) Location / social factors from social.yaml: crime_rate,
            park_proximity_m, transit_score, school_rating (weight: 15 %)
     - Draft assessed values are produced for all 20 parcels.
     - Equity review: sales ratio studies ensure no zone is assessed
       above 100 % of market value.

   Year 3 — Notice, Appeal & Certification
     - Notices of Assessment mailed to all owners of record by March 1.
     - Appeal window: March 1 – April 30 (60 days).
     - Certified roll delivered to the Tax Collector by June 1.
     - Tax bills issued July 1; due in two instalments (Oct 1 / Mar 1).

   Between cycles, a supplemental assessment is triggered when:
     - A deed is recorded (price evidence updates AV proportionally).
     - A building permit for > 10 % area change is closed.
     - A flood event changes the flood_risk flag.

3. Annual Cadence (within a cycle year)

   Jan–Feb    Field data collection / deed reconciliation
   Mar        Notices of Assessment mailed
   Mar–Apr    Appeal hearings (Informal Review Board)
   May        Final Assessment Roll published (public record)
   Jun        Certified roll → Tax Collector
   Jul        Tax bills issued
   Oct 1      First instalment due
   Mar 1      Second instalment due

4. Public Obligations

   Open Records
     - The Assessment Roll (parcel_id, address, AV, owner of record) is
       a public document, updated each cycle.
     - sales_history prices disclosed under the Land Registration
       Ordinance are public once recorded.

   Public Inspection Period
     - The draft roll is open for inspection for 30 days before notices
       are mailed.  Any resident may review any parcel's AV and the
       methodology used to derive it.

   Annual Report
     - The Chief Assessor publishes an Annual Assessment Report covering:
       total taxable value by zone, number of exemptions granted,
       appeal outcomes, and the sales ratio study results.

5. Appeal Process

   Tier 1 — Informal Review (Assessor's Office)
     - Owner submits a written request with supporting evidence
       (comparable sales, independent appraisal, building condition
       photos) within the 60-day window.
     - Deputy Assessor reviews and may adjust AV administratively.
     - Decision issued within 15 business days.

   Tier 2 — Assessment Review Board (ARB)
     - Independent three-member board appointed by the Municipal Council.
     - Owner may appeal a Tier 1 denial within 30 days.
     - Hearing scheduled within 45 days; owner and Assessor both present
       evidence.
     - Board may reduce, confirm, or (rarely) increase AV.
     - Decision is binding on the Assessor unless further appealed.

   Tier 3 — Tralaland Superior Court
     - Owner may appeal an ARB decision within 60 days on a question of
       law or on grounds that AV exceeds market value by > 10 %.
     - Court may appoint an independent referee.

   Appeal does not stay the tax bill; owner pays under protest and
   receives a refund (with interest at 4 % p.a.) if AV is reduced.

6. Exemption Process

   Step 1 — Application
     - Owner submits Form AX-1 (Homestead) or AX-2 (Non-Profit/Civic)
       to the Registry & Records Unit by February 1 of the assessment
       year.
     - Required attachments:
         Homestead: proof of residency (utility bill, voter registration)
                    matching the parcel address.
         Non-Profit: certificate of incorporation, proof of qualifying use.

   Step 2 — Review
     - GIS & Data Unit cross-checks owner_id against social.yaml and
       verifies occupancy in field inspection records.
     - Conflicting ownership (e.g. a company holds a parcel claimed as
       homestead) triggers a deficiency notice; owner has 15 days to cure.

   Step 3 — Determination
     - Approved exemptions are applied to the draft AV before notices
       are mailed.
     - Denied applications carry a 30-day appeal right directly to the
       Assessment Review Board (skips Tier 1).

   Step 4 — Annual Renewal
     - Homestead exemptions require a one-page renewal by February 1
       each year confirming continued owner-occupancy.
     - Failure to renew results in full AV assessment and a back-tax
       calculation for the lapsed year.


SAMPLE TAX CALCULATIONS (illustrative, current cycle)
------------------------------------------------------

  P1 — 12 Park Lane (owner O10, Priya Nair, residential)
    Last sale:  520,000 TRL (2019)
    AV:         540,000 TRL  (3 % appreciation adjustment)
    Homestead:  -81,000 TRL  (15 % reduction)
    Taxable AV: 459,000 TRL
    Base tax:   459,000 × 0.0125 = 5,737 TRL/yr

  P3 — 50 Main Street (owner O3, Delpaso Holdings, commercial)
    Last sale:  1,450,000 TRL (2020)
    AV:         1,560,000 TRL
    No exemption (company, commercial use)
    Taxable AV: 1,560,000 TRL
    Base tax:   1,560,000 × 0.0125 = 19,500 TRL/yr

  P19 — 22 North Road (owner O8, Tomas Lindgren, mixed-use)
    Last sale:  2,276,000 TRL (2024) — most recent arm's-length deed
    AV:         2,276,000 TRL  (sale year = assessment year)
    No homestead (mixed-use zoning)
    Flood surcharge applies: × (0.0125 + 0.0015) = 0.014
    Tax:        2,276,000 × 0.014 = 31,864 TRL/yr
